Component & rig authoring guide

Plain-language instructions for rebuilding a theme's mascot as a rig and authoring hats, eyewear, and held items — written so the theme-builder skill (or a person) can follow them without knowing the app's code.

1 · The rig file: one SVG, named groups

A rigged mascot is a single SVG. The app finds the parts it can animate by their id. Draw everything in the classic 24×24 box the flat mascots already use — same body, same coordinates.

<svg viewBox="0 0 24 24">
  <g id="rig-root">                     ← breathing happens here (the app adds it)
    <g id="rig-arm-left"  data-pivot="2.5 9">  …arm art…  </g>
    <g id="rig-arm-right" data-pivot="21.5 9"> …arm art…  <g id="slot-item"/> </g>
    <g id="rig-leg-left"  data-pivot="8.95 17"> …leg art… </g>
    <g id="rig-leg-right" data-pivot="15.05 17">…leg art… </g>
    <g id="rig-body">
      …the capsule…
      <g id="rig-face-idle">…</g>
      <g id="rig-face-welcome" style="display:none">…</g>
      <g id="rig-face-curious" style="display:none">…</g>
      <g id="rig-face-shocked" style="display:none">…</g>
      <g id="rig-face-dizzy"   style="display:none">…</g>
      <g id="rig-face-blink"   style="display:none">…</g>
    </g>
    <g id="slot-hat"/>      <g id="slot-eyewear"/>
  </g>
</svg>
  • Only rig-body is required. Missing parts just don't animate.
  • Limbs are drawn hanging down from their pivot. The pose data assumes it.
  • data-pivot="x y" marks the hinge (shoulder, hip) in viewBox coordinates. If omitted, the app uses the top-center of the group.
  • Every face group except the default starts style="display:none".
  • Optional side-peek grip hands: rig-hand-peek-right / rig-hand-peek-left — simple mitten stubs (no fingers — match the limb shapes). During a side peek the renderer clones this art and pins it OVER the screen edge, outside the body's lean transform, so the hands stay planted on the frame while the head tips out. Clicking the peeking buddy swings him out around the edge.
2 · Faces are paint, not holes

The flat mascots cut the eyes out of the body with fill-rule="evenodd" so the desktop shows through. A rig can't do that — a swappable face has to be its own group. So: keep the body solid, and paint the eyes on top in a dark "socket" color (Golden Sunbreak already ships one: #2a1004; Halftone uses #1e2636).

Ship one group per expression. The full set the app understands today: idle, welcome, curious, shocked, dizzy, blink. Blink is just the idle face with the eyes drawn as closed lines — the app flashes it for ~120 ms every 6–12 s.

Things that belong to every expression (whiskers, a nose) go inside each face group, so nothing lingers when faces swap.

3 · Component slots: hats, eyewear, held items

Slots are empty groups at fixed anchor points. A component is a small piece of SVG art drawn around its anchor in the same 24×24 space — drop it in the slot and it fits every capsule buddy automatically.

Tint components with the theme tokens (var(--rig-accent) etc.) when they should adapt per theme, or hardcode colors when the component has its own identity (a gold crown stays gold). Theme signatures can ship as components: Halftone's dimensional visor is eyewear, Kuromi's jester hat and Strawberry Kitty's ears-and-bow are hats — which means any theme can borrow them.

4 · Tinting: how colors resolve

Rigs are inlined into the page (after sanitizing), so CSS variables and currentColor finally work — unlike today's <img> path, where currentColor silently resolves to black (that's why Halftone's body ships black right now).

  • var(--rig-accent) — theme accent. Default body tint for themes without custom art.
  • var(--rig-on-accent) — face color on accent-tinted bodies.
  • var(--rig-line) — outline color for white-body mascots (maps to the theme's fg).
  • Hardcoded colors are fine and encouraged where they ARE the identity (Golden Sunbreak's #f0a828 amber, Kuromi's #FF4FB8 pink).
5 · What the app animates for free (never author these)
  • Poses — arm/leg rotations per state (wave, shocked arms-up, peek grip) are app data applied to your groups.
  • Drag physics — limbs trail the body on springs while dragging and wobble on release.
  • Breathing, blinking, greet hop, drop bounce, 3D tilt — wrapper- and group-level, automatic.
  • Scene companions — flourish elements (sun, motes, sparkles, glitch bars, the chromatic ghost) are satellites that spring-follow the buddy at a preferred offset. A theme declares them as small SVGs + an offset; the physics is app-side.
  • Reduced effects — the a11y setting turns all of the above off; your art must look right standing still.

Because behaviors are app data, every new app version teaches every existing rig new tricks with no re-authoring.

6 · Safety rules (the sanitizer will strip these)

Rig SVGs are third-party content inlined into the app, so they pass through a sanitizer. Don't use: <script>, <style>, <foreignObject>, SMIL animation tags (<animate>…), any on*= attribute, or external URLs. Only same-document #refs and data:image/* URLs survive — embedded raster art is allowed, remote fetches are not.
